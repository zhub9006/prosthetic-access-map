# Identified Coverage Gaps

## Gap 1: No Prosthetists/Orthotists in Rural Service Areas

All three regions — Rural West Virginia, Appalachian Eastern Kentucky, and the Mississippi Delta — have **zero** prosthetic or orthotic specialists within a 50km radius. This means residents cannot access:
- Initial prosthetic fitting and prescription
- Ongoing prosthetic alignment and adjustments
- Orthotic fabrication and fitting
- Prosthetic maintenance and repair
- Gait training and rehabilitation

## Gap 2: General Healthcare Cannot Substitute for Specialized Care

While general clinics, pharmacies, and hospitals exist in all regions, these facilities lack:
- Certified prosthetists (CP) or orthotists (CO)
- Custom prosthetic/orthotic fabrication equipment
- Specialized seating and mobility clinics
- Billing expertise for prosthetic reimbursement (Medicare, Medicaid, private insurance)

## Gap 3: Trial-Patient Disconnect

With only 17.6% of prosthetic clinical trials actively recruiting and 42.7% already completed, there is a disconnect between:
- The **maturity of prosthetic research** (926 completed trials)
- The **current lack of recruitment** (381 recruiting, 430 unknown status)
- The **geographic inaccessibility** of remaining trial sites for rural populations

This suggests that rural residents are doubly disadvantaged: they lack both local prosthetic care AND easy access to clinical trial participation.

## Gap 4: Transportation and Poverty Barriers

- **Rural West Virginia**: Average median household income ~$52,000; limited public transit
- **Appalachian Eastern KY**: Average median household income ~$48,000; high rates of disability; limited public transit
- **Mississippi Delta**: Average median household income ~$45,000; highest poverty rates in the US; limited public transit

Traveling 60–150 km for prosthetic care represents a significant financial and logistical burden.

## Recommended Interventions

1. **Mobile Prosthetic Clinics**: Deploy fitting and adjustment services on a rotating schedule
2. **Telehealth Prosthetic Consultations**: Remote alignment checks and provider referrals
3. **Travel Voucher Programs**: Subsidize trips to urban prosthetic centers
4. **Local Training Programs**: Train healthcare workers in basic prosthetic maintenance
5. **Clinical Trial Outreach**: Partner with trials to include rural recruitment sites
6. **Medicaid Expansion**: Ensure prosthetic coverage in all states (MS and KY have partial expansions; WV has full expansion)
